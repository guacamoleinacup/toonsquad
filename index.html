How It Works
💰 Entry & Budget
$64 Entry Fee
Each player pays $64 to enter.
$60 is your auction bidding budget — all $60 must be spent.
The remaining $32 (extra $4 × 8 players) goes as a bonus to whoever drafts the tournament champion.
🏀 Draft Rules
• Everyone must own exactly 8 teams at the end of the auction.
• #15 & #16 seeds are picked clockwise in order for $0.50 each before bidding begins.
• Minimum bid is $0.50.
• Players take turns nominating a team and must bid at least $0.50 on their own nomination.
• Once you Pass or declare done bidding on a team, you cannot jump back in.
📊 Budget Constraint
You must always keep enough money to fill your remaining roster spots at the $0.50 minimum.

Max bid formula:
$ Left − ($0.50 × empty spots remaining)

Example: $60 budget, 0 teams → max first bid = $56.50
🏆 Winning Money
When your team wins a game, you earn the losing team's bid amount.

Example: Michigan State ($30) vs Wisconsin ($20). Wisconsin wins →
Wisconsin owner banks +$30
Wisconsin's $20 bid stays at risk — that's what the next opponent would earn if they beat Wisconsin.
🎯 Championship Bonus
Whoever owns the tournament champion wins an additional $32 bonus on top of all their regular game winnings.

They also get their original auction bid returned — since the champion is the last team standing and never "loses" to anyone, their owner gets that money back.

Example: if you drafted the champion for $20, you earn all your game winnings + $32 bonus + $20 bid back. This bonus comes from the $4 extra per player in the entry fee (8 × $4 = $32).
📝 Quick Example
SKRAEMS owns Florida ($31). BEAN owns Clemson ($2.50).

If Clemson upsets Florida:
BEAN banks +$31 🤑

If Florida beats Clemson:
SKRAEMS banks +$2.50
Florida ($31) remains at risk for their next game.
